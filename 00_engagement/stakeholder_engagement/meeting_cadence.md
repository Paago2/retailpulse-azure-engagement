# Meeting Cadence

## Project

RetailPulse Azure Data Engagement Platform

## Purpose

This document defines the meeting cadence for the RetailPulse Azure data modernization engagement.

The goal is to keep executive stakeholders, business owners, and technical teams aligned throughout delivery.

---

## Meeting Cadence Summary

| Meeting | Frequency | Participants | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Steering Update | Biweekly | Executive Sponsor, Engagement Lead, Tech Lead | Review roadmap, risks, milestones, budget, and decisions |
| Sprint Planning | Every 2 weeks | Engagement Lead, Tech Lead, Delivery Team | Confirm sprint goals, backlog scope, capacity, and ownership |
| Daily Standup | Daily or 3x weekly | Delivery Team | Review progress, blockers, and next actions |
| Technical Architecture Review | Weekly during design/build | Tech Lead, Cloud Engineer, Data Engineers, Security | Review architecture decisions, dependencies, and implementation approach |
| Business Requirements Review | Weekly during discovery/reporting phases | Business Owners, BI Lead, Business Analyst | Validate KPIs, dashboard requirements, and business definitions |
| Governance Review | Weekly or as needed | Governance Lead, Security, Data Engineering, BI | Review data quality, access, RLS, lineage, and policy concerns |
| Sprint Review / Demo | Every 2 weeks | Stakeholders and Delivery Team | Demonstrate completed work and gather feedback |
| Sprint Retrospective | Every 2 weeks | Delivery Team | Identify what went well, what needs improvement, and process actions |
| Risk and Escalation Review | Weekly or as needed | Engagement Lead, Tech Lead, Impacted Owners | Review active risks, blockers, and escalation items |

---

## Recommended Sprint Rhythm

The project uses a two-week sprint cycle.

### Week 1

- Sprint planning
- Task assignment
- Architecture/design work
- Technical implementation
- Risk review

### Week 2

- Complete implementation
- Testing and validation
- Documentation updates
- Sprint review/demo
- Retrospective
- Planning for next sprint

---

## Status Update Format

Each recurring status update should answer:

1. What was completed?
2. What is in progress?
3. What is blocked?
4. What decisions are needed?
5. What risks need attention?
6. What is planned next?

---

## Leadership Interpretation

A good meeting cadence prevents surprises.

The Engagement & Technical Lead should use meetings to maintain alignment, surface blockers early, protect delivery scope, and connect technical progress to business outcomes.
